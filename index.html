Jay-Jay Okocha

The man born as a footballer

Here's a time line of Jay-Jay Okocha's life:

  • 1973 born in Enugu, Enugu State, Nigeria
  • 1990 had a glimpse of limelight when the local club Enugu Rangers signed him
  • 1991 joined Eintracht Frankfurt, and continued to showcase his rare talent and skills there. He scored the goal of the season against goalkeeping legend Oliver Kahn, after manoeuvring past several players.
  • 1995 transferred to Turkish top division side Fernabahce for a few of 1 million pounds. During his spell at the club, he racked up 30 goals in 62 appearances, mostly direct free kick goals. He became synonymous with spot kick goals. He was given a Turkish citizenship during his time at the club.
  • 1998 Paris Saint Germaine splashed out 14 million pounds on the Nigerian midfielder making him Africa's most expensive player that year. He spent four years at the French capital, making 84 appearances and scoring 12 goals.
  • 2002joined Bolton Wanderers on a free transfer. The euphoria of his first season at the club was dampened by prolonged injuries, despite this setback, the Nigerian footballer helped save his side from relegation by scoring seven crucial goals.
  • 2003 Okocha was given the captain role and arm band following his continued display of passion and leadership.
  • 2006 moved onto Qatar after rejecting a contract extension offer from Bolton Wanderers.
  • 2007 returned to England after one year in Qatar to join Hull City on a free transfer . He was a member of the team that won promotion to the premier league after countless attempts in 104 years.
  • 2008retired at the end of the season due to prolonged injuries and fitness concerns
  • 2015 Okocha was voted as the Chairman of Delta State Football Association.
